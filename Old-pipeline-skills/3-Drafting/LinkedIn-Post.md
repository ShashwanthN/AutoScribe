# LinkedIn Post Content Type

LinkedIn posts need a fast entry point, one memorable idea, and enough specificity to reward the click. In this system, LinkedIn structure is decided before voice adaptation.

## Content Goal

Create a compact argument, story, observation, or practical takeaway that can stand alone in a feed.

The draft should define:

- the hook idea
- the reader problem or curiosity gap
- the core point
- the supporting proof or example
- the takeaway
- the CTA or discussion prompt


## Drafting Requirements

The Phase 3 draft should be WHAT-only and should include:

- 3-5 hook directions, not polished final hooks
- the exact idea each paragraph or bullet must carry
- proof points and examples to include
- transitions between beats
- the intended emotional/argument progression
- approximate length, usually 120-350 words unless instructed otherwise

## Guardrails

- Do not write in the selected voice.
- Do not produce final LinkedIn prose in Phase 3.
- Do not invent metrics.
- Do not add a CTA that does not match the content promise.
- Do not let the hook overpower the actual argument.
