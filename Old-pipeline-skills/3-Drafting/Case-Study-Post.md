# Case Study Content Type

A case study should make a result credible by showing the starting problem, the mechanism of change, the evidence, and the transferable lesson.

## Content Goal

Create a case study that is specific enough to trust and useful beyond the featured example.

The draft should define:

- subject and context
- initial problem
- stakes
- chosen intervention or approach
- implementation details
- measurable or observable outcome
- lesson for similar readers

## Structure

```markdown
# Case Study Structure

## Working Title
[Outcome-led, plain.]

## Subject
Company/person/team:
Industry/context:
Reader relevance:

## Challenge
Initial state:
Operational/business consequence:
Why existing approaches were insufficient:

## Approach
What changed:
Why this approach:
Important decisions:
Constraints:

## Execution
Steps taken:
Timeline:
Roles/systems involved:
Friction or tradeoffs:

## Results
Quantitative outcomes:
Qualitative outcomes:
Timeframe:
Attribution caveats:

## Insight
Transferable lesson:
What readers should do differently:
```

## Drafting Requirements

The Phase 3 draft should include:

- exact claims supported by available evidence
- missing metrics clearly marked as missing
- before/after comparisons
- mechanism of change, not just result claims
- caveats where attribution is uncertain
- reusable lesson for the target reader

## Guardrails

- Do not invent metrics.
- Do not turn the case study into an ad.
- Do not apply voice.
- Do not write final prose.
- Do not hide uncertainty.
