# Use Case Content Type

A use-case piece should help the reader recognize a situation, understand why it happens, and see what approach fits. It should read as diagnosis first, application second.

## Content Goal

Create a piece that explains a recurring organizational or workflow problem and the approach that addresses it.

The draft should define:

- the situation or use case
- who experiences it
- the root cause
- why common fixes fail
- the recommended approach
- outcome signals
- practical boundaries

## Structure

```markdown
# Use Case Structure

## Working Title
[Plain description of the use case and outcome.]

## Situation
Who experiences this:
Where it shows up:
Trigger conditions:

## Diagnosis
Surface problem:
Root cause:
Why it persists:

## Cost
Operational cost:
Strategic/customer/team cost:
What happens if ignored:

## Reframe
Old way of seeing it:
Better way of seeing it:

## Approach
Principles:
Workflow/system changes:
Decision criteria:

## Outcome Signals
Early indicators:
Long-term indicators:
Risks or caveats:

## Closing
Reader takeaway:
Next step:
```

## Drafting Requirements

The Phase 3 draft should include:

- concrete use-case boundaries
- root-cause reasoning
- examples of the problem in practice
- what the approach is and is not
- signs that the approach is working
- cases where the approach may not fit

## Guardrails

- Do not lead with product promotion.
- Do not skip diagnosis.
- Do not apply voice.
- Do not write final prose.
- Do not claim outcomes without evidence.
