### Market First

I watched a team build a perfect AI tool. It had top engineers , a great interface. Sent it out to labs.

A month goes by. Silence.

The feedback? Simple. "We just need report writers , not a new tool."

If you build it , they will come is a line from a movie. Real life doesn't work like that.

Same AI engine. A random chat at a conference changed it all.

We shifted from research labs to manufacturing quality checks. The code didn't change. Just the crowd.

Building a shiny thing before you check if people actually hurt - that is the most expensive move.

The standard fixes don't work:
- Building faster speeds up the wrong path.
- More cash is just a bigger bet on a guess.
- A sample tests your fix , not the pain.

Here is what worked for a flooring idea.

I scraped forums. Found hundreds of guys with failing garage floors. Asked the tool to find the common thread. It spit out the main bunch: tough home garage coatings.

Tested a few messages. One stuck.

Ran a small post. A lot of people wanted in.

Once you have the chatter , look for this:
- How many people share this exact pain? A handful is a hobby.
- How mad are they? Desperation sounds different.
- What do they say they'd pay?
- Is it a trend or a loud person?

I use a tool to map out the whole structure. Guide here. It helps me see if the pain is wide enough.

I watch the crowd shape the product. Building for a specific scream is easier than a vague whisper.

I ask a hard question. Would a stranger hand over cash in thirty seconds for this fix? If no - it's a solution looking for a problem.

You can dig for a weekend and save a year of building.

i have a simple check before I build anything.
- Scrape the complaints.
- Ask the tool to group them.
- Test your message.
- Confirm the crowd.

Resist the building instinct. I know the crowd always wins.

This whole thing is simple Af.

TL;DR: Check the pain first. The crowd always wins.
