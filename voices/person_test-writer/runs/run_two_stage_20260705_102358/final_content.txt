I spent a while making a tool. I thought it was really polished.

A bunch of people in a specific field seemed like the right fit. They took a look.

Nothing happened. No one used it.

I called around. A few of them said the same thing.

"We just need someone to write the reports , not a new tool."

I used to think speed was the whole game. If i just built it fast enough and made it shine , the rest would take care of itself.

Yeah i get the appeal of just building first - the quiet confidence is nice , but the room is what decides.

My polished thing just sat there. I aimed it at the wrong crowd.

The silence was loud Af.

I was at a get-together. Someone started venting about a totally different headache.

Their team kept throwing money away on something that kept breaking. I realized the engine of my tool could look at that new thing just as well.

Nothing changed in the code. I just pointed it at a different room and called it something else.

That detour cost me a bit. It cost me a few months and a bunch of morale.

I started watching what people were already yelling about online.

- I pulled a bunch of conversations.
- I ran them through a thing to find the pattern.
- Then i tried out a few different ways of describing what i was thinking of building.
- One description got a way stronger reaction.
- I looked up where those people hung out.

It took a bit of cash and a handful of days. It felt way better than building the whole thing and watching it sit there.

### What am i actually looking for? (in my POV)

- I looked at how often the exact same complaint showed up.
- I checked the feel of the language. I could see if they were desperate or just curious.
- I checked if they already threw money at other fixes.
- I tried to see if it was a loud few or a big crowd.

The thing didn't have to stay what i first imagined. I threw out whole chunks of it that didn't match the main headache i saw.

I called it something different and pointed it at a different group. It was easier to make something for a sharp pinch than a general vague feeling.

- I learned to ask a hard question.
- If i stripped away all the excitement and the story , i could see if this actually mattered to them.
- If the answer was no , it was smarter to stop right there.
- A weekend of wondering is cheaper than a long detour.

So before i touch a single bit of building now , i do a quick check.

- I write down the rough idea.
- I see if the pain is real out in the wild.
- I test the pitch.
- I see who cares.

It sounds like extra steps. It saves me from throwing a party no one shows up to.

i found that waiting and checking first is the real path.
