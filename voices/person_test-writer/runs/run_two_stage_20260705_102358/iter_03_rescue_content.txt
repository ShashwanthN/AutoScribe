### The Hook

You build a perfect thing. Top engineers , clean UI , a bunch of funding.
Free trials to top labs. Weeks later , zero active users.
"We just need report writers , not a new tool."
Yeah i get it - the founder thought speed and capital were enough. The market had other plans. The gap between believing you are right and proving it isn't about grit. It's about checking.

### The Indictment

People love the myth. Founder coding through the night. It's the plot of a hundred startup movies - doesn't work that way.
I don't buy it. The right crowd comes first. Building is just the price of entry.
You can't code a market into existence. That Fracto team had everything. Zero results. The signal was loud Af.
I saw the opposite too. Weaker team , jankier version , people wanting it. The difference wasn't the code. It was the crowd.

### The Turning Point

Chance conference conversation. Quality engineer talking about scrapping millions.
- Before: Research labs. Zero.
- After: Manufacturing QA. Valued.
The core insight - the AI engine didn't change. What changed was the crowd and the message.
That detour took months. Burned morale. Ate budget.

### Validate Before You Build

Most expensive mistake i see is spending on a polished version before checking if the problem is real.
The principle - check the problem organically before writing real code.
Here is what i did with FloorOps. Trend on Exploding Topics for epoxy floor complaints. Scraped Reddit with Apify. Ran it through GPT. Results clustered around "Military-grade coatings for home garages."
Tested messages on PickFu. One over-indexed. Confirmed the crowd on SparkToro. Spent a small amount on ads. Got a bunch of waitlist names. Then i built.
Tools make it fast. A short time , not a lot of money. The advantage goes to the people who check first.
The objection is spending cash on real usage data. The difference - testing your solution to a guessed problem isn't the same as checking if the pain is real. Scraping validates the pain itself.

### How to Analyse Market Signals

Once you have the raw data , the real work starts.
I look at how often the problem pops up. Frequent enough? I look at the language. Desperation versus optimization.
When the signal gets complex , i use draw.io MCP to map it out.
The final test - loud minority versus real crowd.

### Shape the Idea Around the Market

The crowd shapes the product. Not the other way around.
Fracto's tech was solid - the application had to switch. Research to QA.
FloorOps didn't build a general tool. We built for that niche.
The lesson - easier to build for a pinched nerve than a vague ache.

### When to Walk Away (in my POV)

The hardest question. If i had to sell this to a stranger , with no founder charm , would they buy it? Or is it a solution looking for a problem?
A weekend of checking is cheaper than months of failure. Not checking is failing.
The cost of waiting - a weekend of checking beats the cost of a detour.

### The Closing

i spent years thinking the right move was building fast to find the right crowd.
Now i think the right move is checking fast to avoid wasting a build.
i write down the who , the what's broken , and how they solve it now. Scrape the problem space. Poll the message. Confirm the crowd.
The instinct to build first is strong. The advantage belongs to the patient founder.
The market always wins.

TL;DR: Check the problem exists before you build the solution. A weekend of checking beats months of failure.
